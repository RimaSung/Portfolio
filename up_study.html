RESEARCH

Who are the target customers?

Based on data pulled by the CRM system and results from the focus group, we noticed:

  • The average age of our customers is 18-30 women

  • 80% of them have a daytime job or schoolwork

  • Some of them come with their parents for consultation

  • 80% of customers read study abroad information on social media, Wikipedia, or school websites before the consultation

  • 70% of customers want to apply for a Master's degree

What are their end goals?

Even though our customers read study abroad information on the internet, they believe a consultant can provide professional advice and help to choose the right school based on customers study/work experience and English skills.

Why do they need consult service?

Customers indicate that collecting information is time-consuming since specific application requirements vary by program, degree, and school. They are exhausted after work in the daytime, so they want an assistant to organize the data or guide them to apply for school.

What are customers' expectations?

  • Gather needed study abroad information on a signal site

  • Spend less effort and time preparing the application works

  • Have an online consultation

Other insights

  • They cannot find detailed information about fees and services on our website

  • Take time to find our website on the google search result page

  • They cannot find contact information on the website

  • Chose an agency based on other customers' reviews and experience

SOLUTIONS

Feature 01

Contact form

According to the research, many customers cannot find the contact information on the website. Therefore, A new contact form was designed at the bottom of each page that motivates customers to take further action after reading related information on the website. Customers are required to fill out the form with their name, phone number, and choice of contact office from a drop-down menu. Once customers submit the request, the information will be recorded on the Enterprise Resource Planning Management System and will inform the consultant via email.

Feature 02

Customers reviews and success case stories

Since many customers read articles about application experience for collecting information, we believe a Word-Of-Mouth marketing strategy is the best way to build a good impression on customers. Once customers share their great experience using our service, it will encourage consumer-to-consumer communication. Thus, customer reviews and success case stories are displayed on the index page to attract potential customers to our service.

Feature 03

Articles about schools and tags

According to the report, many customers would like to gather needed study abroad information on a signal site to save time. After further observation, we learned that customers often use location, degree offered, and characteristics as search keywords when collecting school information. Based on these insights, we designed the article session to meet the needs of our customers.

Moreover, customers could retrieve information by selecting tags on some pages that aid customers to recall memory and encourage them to keep interacting with the website. By doing so, 66% of the bounce rate was reduced and impacted SEO ranking.

Feature 04

Updated articles

To make customers find and aware of our service on the first page of search engine results, we decided to open a blog channel that provides updated information for customers. It is also friendly to the search engine to promote our website since it can: (1) meet search intent by introducing search keywords in the article. (2) encourage customers to visit other pages by adding internal links. (3) attract customers by providing more graphics, visuals, and illustrations in the article.
